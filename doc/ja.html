chron

other chronology such as season, quarter, etc.

Other forms of enumeration, like "Spring" or "1st quarter" should be carried in this data element, unnormalized. Where numeric dates are also available, place the numeric portion in the "date" data element. So a recorded date of publication of "Spring, 1992" becomes date="1992"&chronology="Spring". These can also be expressed as "ssn" for the season and "quarter" for the normalized quarter (1|2|3|4), which are included for backwards compatibility to v 0.1.

issue

number of the issue

This is the number of the published issue of a journal, corresponding to the actual physical piece in most cases. While usually numeric, it could be non-numeric. Note that some publications use chronology in the place of enumeration, i.e. Spring, 1998.

artnum

article number assigned by publisher

Article numbers are often generated for publications that do not have usable pagination, in particular electronic journal articles. This data element can also be used for book components that have chapter numbers.

A URL may be the only usable identifier for an online article.

eissn

ISSN for electronic version

Although there is no distinction by format in the assignment of ISSNs, some bibliographic services now carry both the ISSN for the paper version and a separate ISSN for the electronic version. This data element is included here to allow the OpenURL to carry both ISSNs and distinguish them.
